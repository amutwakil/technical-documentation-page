Introduction

League of Legends is a hugely popular MOBA (multiplayer online battle arena) with an active player base of 80 million people. A "MOBA" is a subgenre of strategy video games in which each player controls a single character with a set of unique abilities that improve over the course of a game and which contribute to the team's overall strategy.

In League of Legends, players assume the role of a "champion" with 4 abilities varying around their class and battle against a team of other player- or computer-controlled champions.

As of August 2020, there are a total of 150 champions. The classes themselves are divided into 5 categories (each containing a potential subcategory):

  • Tanks
  • Bruisers
  • Mages
  • Assassins
  • Marksman

Objective of the game

The objective the game is relatively simple: destroy the enemy nexus.

This however is not so easily achieved as the nexus is defended not only by the enemy team, but also by structures known as "towers" that deal tremendous amounts of damage to the player under certain circumstances:

  • If the player causes damage to the enemy player while in "tower range" the player will begin to receive "tower aggro". Evey strike from the tower causes additional subsequent damage. In the earlier stages of the game this can be devastating.
  • In the event a player approaches a tower without any accompanying minions or "creeps" (which will be explained momentarily)

There are a number of "neutral objectives" which come in the form of "monsters" that can be killed to garner some type of advantage known as a "buff" (either in the form of statistical attributes to themselves, their entire team, or their minions.)
